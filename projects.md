# Projects

In my research, I focus on humanitarian and developmental efforts in the DPR Korea from the perspective of institutional logics. I am interested in how organizations navigate institutional complexity in restrictive environments, and I also engage with topics surrounding state propaganda and migration through research and teaching. My current work is organized around my dissertation project.

### I. Under Pressure: An Institutional Analysis of UNICEF and WFP Responses to Humanitarian Dilemmas in the DPRK

<div class="meta-block" markdown="0">
<dl>
<dt>Status</dt><dd>Active (doctoral dissertation project)</dd>
<dt>Duration</dt><dd>2024&ndash;present</dd>
<dt>Researcher</dt><dd>Robin Christopher Brehm (University of Vienna)</dd>
<dt>Supervision</dt><dd>Univ.-Prof. Mag. Dr. Rüdiger Frank</dd>
</dl>
</div>

How did UNICEF and the World Food Programme respond to humanitarian dilemmas during the implementation of international assistance in the DPRK (1995&ndash;2020)? Humanitarian organizations routinely face dilemmas: situations in which core obligations conflict and every available option violates at least one binding commitment. Although organizations face very similar pressures, their responses differ.

Drawing on the institutional logics perspective within new institutional theory, this study conducts an in-depth, longitudinal comparative analysis of WFP and UNICEF operations in North Korea. It examines three dilemmas (access, complicity, and independence/instrumentalization) to explain how international organizations manage to operate with, and despite, dilemmas in politically sensitive settings.
