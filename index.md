I am a predoctoral university assistant at the Department of East Asian Studies at the University of Vienna, where I administer the master's program East Asian Economy and Society (EcoS) and teach at the graduate level. I also serve as Vice-Director of the [European Centre for North Korean Studies (ECNK)](https://ecnk.univie.ac.at/), where I manage public outreach, organize academic events, and maintain a directory and network of field specialists. I hold an M.A. in East Asian Economy and Society from the University of Vienna, an M.A. in Development Studies from the University of Passau, and a B.A. in Political and Social Studies from the Julius-Maximilians-University of Würzburg.
{: .page-lead }

My research examines how humanitarian organizations operate in restrictive environments, with a regional focus on East Asia and the Korean Peninsula. In my dissertation, "Under Pressure: Institutional Complexity in United Nations Assistance to the DPRK," I study how the World Food Programme and UNICEF responded to humanitarian dilemmas during the implementation of assistance in North Korea between 1995 and 2020, drawing on the institutional logics perspective within new institutional theory. Beyond humanitarian action, my interests include political propaganda and state media in East Asia as well as the integration of post-communist citizens in divided societies. Methodologically, I work with qualitative content analysis, interviews, and survey-based designs.

At the University of Vienna, I teach graduate courses on humanitarian assistance and political propaganda in East Asia. Before joining the department, I worked as a researcher for Korea Risk Group's [DPRK Reports](https://dprk-reports.org/), as a research assistant at the LBG Open Innovation in Science Center, and completed research stays with NK News and the Hanns Seidel Foundation in Seoul.
