I am a predoctoral university assistant at the Department of East Asian Studies at the University of Vienna, where I administer the master's program [East Asian Economy and Society (EcoS)](https://ecos.univie.ac.at/) and teach graduate-level courses. I also serve as Vice-Director of the [European Centre for North Korean Studies](https://ecnk.univie.ac.at/), where I manage public outreach, organize academic events, and maintain a directory and network of field specialists. After attaining two M.A. degrees, I decided to commit to a PhD, which has kept me busy since 2024.
{: .page-lead }

In my research, I examine how humanitarian organizations operate in restrictive environments. The dissertation centers on how United Nations Funds and Programmes responded to humanitarian dilemmas they faced while operating in the DPR Korea between 1995 and 2020. The theoretical lens I chose to study these practical challenges is the institutional logics perspective within new institutional theory. Beyond humanitarian action, my research and teaching interests extend to political propaganda, state media messaging, migration, and development. Methodologically, I work with both qualitative and quantitative approaches, although I admit that qualitative research fills me with a tad more excitement.

Before joining the University of Vienna, I worked as a researcher at the LBG Open Innovation in Science Center and for the Korea Risk Group's [DPRK Reports](https://dprk-reports.org/), among others.
