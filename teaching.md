# Teaching

I teach graduate courses at the Department of East Asian Studies, University of Vienna.

## Humanitarian Assistance in East Asia

<div class="meta-block" markdown="0">
<dl>
<dt>Format</dt><dd>VU (lecture with integrated practice), 4 ECTS</dd>
<dt>Level</dt><dd>Graduate (M.A.), University of Vienna</dd>
<dt>Taught</dt><dd>Winter semester 2025/26 and 2026/27</dd>
</dl>
</div>

This course provides an overview of international assistance, covering its key concepts, related theories, implementation challenges, and contemporary debates, with a particular focus on humanitarianism in East Asia. It examines the stakeholders involved in the planning and implementation of assistance programs and their (inter)dependencies, and engages criticisms of humanitarian action through concepts such as dependency, ethical dilemmas, and neocolonialism, applied to East Asian case studies.

## Political Propaganda in Modern East Asia

<div class="meta-block" markdown="0">
<dl>
<dt>Format</dt><dd>VU (lecture with integrated practice), 4 ECTS</dd>
<dt>Level</dt><dd>Graduate (M.A.), University of Vienna</dd>
<dt>Taught</dt><dd>Summer semester 2026</dd>
</dl>
</div>

How do national authorities in East Asia construct, maintain, and repair identity and legitimacy through propaganda? This interactive course decodes propaganda artifacts such as posters, pamphlets, leaflets, paintings, and photographs. In theme-centered sessions spanning the late 19th to the early 21st century, it covers China, Japan, and the Koreas, from empires and wartime propaganda to postwar authoritarianism and liberal democracies.
